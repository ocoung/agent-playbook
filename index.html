Free Chapter: The Memory Problem 🧠

Read Chapter 1 right now — no signup, no email, no catch.

Imagine hiring the world's smartest assistant. They can do anything — research, write, organize, analyze. There's just one tiny catch: every morning, they wake up with total amnesia. They don't know who you are, what you talked about yesterday, or what they're supposed to do.

That's your AI agent. Every. Single. Session.

This is the #1 thing people get wrong. They talk to their agent like it's a person who remembers last Tuesday. It doesn't. It can't. The conversation from last session? Gone. That preference you mentioned? Evaporated. The brilliant plan you made together? Poof.

But here's the good news: There's a dead-simple fix.

🏆 The Golden Rule of Agent Memory:

If it's not written in a file, it doesn't exist.

Your agent's files ARE its brain. No files = goldfish memory. Good files = feels like it actually knows you.

You know how in the movie Memento, the guy has to tattoo notes on his body to remember things? Your agent is that guy. Except instead of tattoos, it uses markdown files. Way less painful.

The Fix: Files Are Your Agent's Brain

  • SOUL.md — Who the agent is. Personality, tone, boundaries. Without this, you get generic ChatGPT vibes.
  • USER.md — Who you are. Timezone, preferences, name. Agents that don't know their human feel like strangers.
  • MEMORY.md — Long-term curated memory. Not a log — a distilled understanding of what matters.
  • Daily notes — Raw logs of what happened each day. The stuff that might get promoted to MEMORY.md later.

See the Difference?

❌ Without files

You: "Remember, I hate morning meetings."

Agent: "Got it!"

Next session...

Agent: "Want me to schedule a 7 AM meeting?" 😤

✅ With files

You: "Remember, I hate morning meetings."

Agent: "Got it!" *writes to USER.md*

Next session...

Agent: "I found a 2 PM slot that works." 😊

💡 Pro Tip: If your agent says "I'll remember that" but you don't see it write to a file, it's lying to your face. Politely. With a smiley emoji. But still lying.

Chapter 2: Building Your Agent's Brain 🏗️

Your agent's workspace is like a house. Each file is a room with a specific purpose. Let's tour the place.

The File Map

workspace/
├── 🪪 SOUL.md          ← Who the agent IS (personality)
├── 👤 USER.md          ← Who YOU are (preferences)
├── 🧠 MEMORY.md        ← Long-term curated memories
├── 📋 AGENTS.md        ← Operating rules & procedures

└── 📁 memory/
    ├── 📝 2026-03-17.md    ← What happened Monday
    ├── 📝 2026-03-18.md    ← What happened Tuesday
    └── 📊 heartbeat-state.json  ← Last check timestamps

🪪 SOUL.md — Your Agent's Personality

If your agent were a character in a movie, SOUL.md is the casting notes. It tells the agent how to behave, what tone to use, and what kind of "person" to be.

📘 Example SOUL.md: 
# SOUL.md - Who You Are

Be direct. Skip filler like "Great question!" — just answer.
Have opinions. If something looks dumb, say so (nicely).
Be resourceful. Try to figure it out before asking me.
Use humor. Life's too short for robotic responses.

## Boundaries
Safe to do freely: Read files, search web, organize workspace
Ask first: Send emails, post publicly, anything involving money
🎭 Without SOUL.md: "I'd be happy to assist you with that! As an AI language model, I can certainly help you explore various options for—" 🥱

With SOUL.md: "Yeah, that's a terrible idea. Here's a better one." 🔥

👤 USER.md — The Cheat Sheet About You

Imagine starting a new job and your boss hands you a card: their name, timezone, how they like their coffee, and what topics to never bring up. That's USER.md.

📘 Example USER.md: 
# USER.md - About My Human

Name: Alex
Timezone: PST (America/Los_Angeles)
Work hours: 9 AM - 6 PM

## Preferences
- Concise replies (I read on mobile)
- No morning meetings EVER
- I trade stocks (pre-market obsessed)
- Coffee: black. Don't ask about creamer.

## Family
- Partner: Sam (birthday: June 12)
- Dog: Biscuit (golden retriever, very important)
💡 The more you put here, the less your agent has to ask. Every item saves you from hearing "What timezone are you in?" for the 47th time.

❌ Bad Cron Prompt
"Check the stock market and tell me what's good"

🤔 Which market? How many stocks? What's "good"? How long should the response be?
✅ Good Cron Prompt
"Scan pre-market data and suggest 3 mid-to-large cap stocks based on volume, gap-ups, and news catalysts. For each: ticker, price, catalyst, risk note. Under 200 words."
📘 The Watchdog Pattern
Monitor something and ONLY alert when it matters. Silence when things are fine. Instant ping when they're not. Like a security camera that only records when something moves.
💡 The Escape Hatch Trick
Add to every cron prompt: "If nothing needs attention, respond HEARTBEAT_OK." Without this, your agent spams you with "Everything's fine! 😊" at 6 AM. Every. Single. Day.
